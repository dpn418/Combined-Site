Basic Privacy

  1. Strong Passwords:
    • A password manager is highly recommended to secure all your passwords to log in for any application.
    • For your password manager, you will also need a strong password that includes text, numbers, and symbols(such as ! or ?). You can test the strength of your password here.
    • This includes making sure that your phone is also locked with a strong password and not a simple PIN.
  2. 2 Factor Authentication(2FA):
    • This is usually done when creating an account by providing a phone number or e-mail to further verify your identity for privacy-sensitive actions such as changing your password. If a site offers the use of Google Authenticator for 2FA, you should use it over a phone number as phone numbers are less secure.
  3. Your Web Browser:
    • Make sure to only use trusted add-ons and extentions.
    • Switch your default web browser to DuckDuckGo or startpage. Both of these search engines do not track any of your data as opposed to Google which does.
  4. Use a VPN
    • Virtual Private Networks help hide your IP address from sites or your Internet Service Provider tracking your activity and it also helps to prevent someone from intercepting your data and viewing it before it reaches you.
  5. Social Media
    • Hide any real information about yourself including your name, address, or phone number. This includes pictures of yourself.
    • Only talk online to people that you know in private settings
    • Manage what permissions social media sites can access such as your location or the ability to send texts. Never give more than what is needed.
