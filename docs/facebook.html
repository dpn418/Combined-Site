The Problem with Facebook

Facebook: "Move Fast and Break Things"

In the beginning, Facebook was simply a cool social website made by chill college students as a way for other college students to communicate and socialze during the Internet-wide bloom of web 2.0. Facebook's founder Mark Zuckerberg promised a great benefit to society to connect people and make the world a more open place. His optomistic message that the Internet and technology could only be here for the betterment of the future society was deeply ingrained in both its users and the employees of Facebook, as Facebook and its user base grew larger and larger. But as Facebook grew and reached a truly global audience, Facebook and its employees continued to focus on growth and reaching even more people with its newsfeed as the main attraction, with that same optomistic change-the-world attitude that left no room for doubts about ethicality. Facebook's newsfeed is driven by an algorithm derived from ample data mined from its now over 2,000,000,000 users to show users the content that they most want to see. This algorithm uses information about user's "likes" and the pages they followed to create an endless source of posts and images curated for the user and the more a user interacted with Facebook, the more precise and targeted the newsfeed will become. All of this content is provided by other users and, due to the sparse legal net that governs information and privacy online, Facebook is not liable for any of the content actually provided on the newsfeed.
